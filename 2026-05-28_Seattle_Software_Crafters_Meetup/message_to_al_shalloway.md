# Message to Al Shalloway (LinkedIn)

## Original draft

Al,

I enjoyed seeing you at last week's event and hearing how your thinking has evolved.
I've always admired how you embrace what works and either evolve or discard the rest
or find something else to fill the gaps.

I also noticed something about how you related to the work, and you even called it out
by saying "You no longer try to be the smartest person in the room". Well, I've always
considered you to be (one of) the smartest people in the room. That hasn't changed, but
there was a self-assurance, an ease, a comfortableness of being in your own skin, that
actually made what you shared even more

Thanks for sharing what you learned and for who you were being.

Best,
Michael

P.S. I'm looking for time-bound, well-defined engagements. I've always been more of a
first follower than thought leader, so if you know any teams who need someone assist
them to adopt tools or thinking, I'd appreciate an introduction.

---

## Suggestions

### Fixes required

- **Missing word** -- second paragraph ends mid-sentence: "even more ___".
  Options: "even more compelling", "even more credible", "even more resonant".

### Phrasing

- "either evolve or discard the rest or find something else to fill the gaps" -- tangled.
  Simplify: "you embrace what works and discard what doesn't."
- "who you were being" -- awkward.
  Use: "how you showed up" or "how you showed up as a person."

### Vulnerability / personal connection

- Consider naming the ~20 years: "I've been watching your thinking evolve for nearly
  twenty years..." -- gives the observation about his mellowing real weight.
- The line about him no longer needing to be the smartest in the room is the heart of
  the vulnerability. Could make your own echo more explicit:
  "I've spent a lot of years trying to earn my place in rooms you were already owning --
  and watching you let that go was unexpectedly freeing."

### The P.S. / subterfuge question

The compliment reads as genuine -- the subterfuge risk is structural, not content.
A P.S. signals "the real ask is here," which retroactively frames everything above it
as softening.

Two options:

1. **Bury the ask in the body** -- weave it in as a natural afterthought, not a tagged-on
   postscript. No architectural signal that the letter was building toward something.

2. **Send two messages** -- a short personal note now; a separate one in a week or two
   that references the first and pivots to the ask. Clean separation of intent.

### P.S. rewrite (if keeping it)

"I'm in a season where I'm looking for ways to contribute -- if you ever hear of a team
that could use a patient implementer rather than another thought leader, I'd welcome
the introduction."

This plays off the "first follower" framing (which is genuinely disarming) and drops
the corporate phrasing ("time-bound, well-defined engagements").
